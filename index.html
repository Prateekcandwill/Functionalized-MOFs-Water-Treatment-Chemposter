Selective Heavy Metal Capture Using Functionalized MOFs

A Breakthrough in Water Purification

INTRODUCTION

What are MOFs?

Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs) are a class of porous materials composed of metal ions connected by organic linkers. Their unique structure provides exceptionally high surface area (up to 10,000 m²/g) and tunable pore sizes, making them ideal for capturing contaminants at the molecular level.

  • Porous MOF with high surface area.
  • High surface area (7,000 m²/g).
  • Tunable pores for selective filtration.

Why it matters:

Toxic heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic) cause 1.8 million deaths annually due to water pollution.

Potential:

  • Scalable clean water solution for 2 billion people lacking safe drinking water (UN).
  • Remove water treatment energy use by 30% vs. traditional methods (ACS).

METHODS

SYNTHESIS

Coat MOFs, Zn, Fe with functionalized linkers for metal adsorption

CHARACTERIZATION

Analyze structure (XRD, IR, BET, TGA) and adsorption capacity

ADSORPTION

Remove 95% of heavy metals (Pb, Hg, As) via batch analysis

REGENERATION

Maintain 90% efficiency over 10 cycles using acid wash

PROTOTYPE

Field-scale MOF filter, achieving 95% removal at 500 L/day

RESULTS

High Adsorption Capacity:

95% removal of heavy metals (Pb, Hg, As): 500 mg/g for Pb, 450 mg/g for Hg

Selectivity:

Functionalized MOF selectively captured heavy metals even in the presence of competing ions, achieving 95% selectivity for lead over calcium

Reusability:

MOFs maintained 90% efficiency over 10 regeneration cycles

Real-World Application:

95% removal of heavy metals at a flow rate of 500 L/day, with projected costs 40% lower than traditional reverse osmosis systems

DISCUSSION

Efficiency and Selectivity:

Functionalized MOFs outperformed traditional adsorbents, achieving 95% removal efficiency and 95% selectivity for heavy metals

Sustainability:

MOF reduced energy consumption by 30% and operational costs by 40%, making it a sustainable alternative to traditional water treatment methods

Scalability:

MOFs sustained >90% efficiency over 10 regeneration cycles

Real-World Application:

The prototype system demonstrated the feasibility of scaling up MOF-based water treatment, with potential applications in both industrial and household settings
